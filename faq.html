What are the rules for Paper Presentation
  • Number of members per team 2
  • During registration, Candidate must give a Hard Copy of abstract along with the payable amount to ISTE office.
  • Abstract must contain Name, Department/year, College name, Contact Details (Cell No.+Email), topic name. Abstract should not be more than 1 page.
  • During the event, candidate must bring the soft copy of Abstract, Slides and Synopsis in a CD. No Pen Drive is allowed. In the case if someone brings in a pen drive, their entries will be discarded.
  • Candidates who have registered themselves can collect the Receipt from the ISTE office in IT Department.
  • Candidate must bring 2 hard copies of Synopsis during the event. One must be submitted at the ISTE office and one must be along the candidates.
  • While making the Slides, One must strictly Follow the following pattern-
    • Font – Times New Roman
    • Title – 18
    • Heading -17
    • Sub heading -16
    • Body content – 14
    • Line Spacing – 1.5 pts.
  • Candidates must bring their Abstracts, slides and Synopsis in Microsoft 97-2003 (.doc) Format only. In the case, if candidates bring in DOCX or PPTX format, they will be responsible for it.
  • Candidates whose abstract are not selected, will be refunded with the cash they paid. They will be intimated either by the Cell Phone or via Email ID.

What are the rules for Scavenger hunt
  • Two different teams from each department.
  • Four teams from first year.
  • Therefore the tally is- 2 * 7 + 4 = 18 teams.
  • The game is divided in basically two rounds.
  • The first round comprises of two slots and second round is the ultimate finale.
  • Each team comprises of maximum 4 members.
  • 9 teams of different department and first year will simultaneously remain active during the first slot of the round and similarly the rest 9 teams in the second slot respectively
  • Out of those two particular slots the winner and runner up from both the slots will be short listed.
  • Now in the second and finale round these top 4 teams will be competing against each other and at the end there will one single winner and a runner up.

What are the rules for Robo Race

CONCEPT:-

RACE of a remote controlled electric motor based machine that can cross over an artificial path crossing through some hurdles, obstacles .

TRACK DETAILS:

  • 1st TRACK: DISCLOSED BEFORE 24 HRS.
  • 2nd  TRACK: DISCLOSED ON TIME.

PROBLEM STATEMENT:-

The participant should build back a manually controlled vehical that can race on tracks of terrain like sand,speed breaker,space elevation etc.The aim of event is to check robustnesss of vehicle and its competence with the opponent on same track.

Machine specification:

  • The machine should be fit in a 30cmx25cmx20cm (Length x Width x height) size of dimension box.
  • Weight of robot should not exceed 4 Kg.
  • The machine should use Maximum 16V  Power Supply .Use of heavy equipment like compressor is banned.
  • The machine can be remote operated using wireless or wired operating system.
  • Readymade machine would not be allowed (Readymade wheels, motor, battery, RF Remote, switch, microcontroller boards, wires can be used).

RULES AND REGULATIONS:-

  • This is racing event so fastest and most balanced robot will win.
  • Robot should be as per the given specifications.
  • Each team can have maximum 3 members.
  • Students from different institutes can form a team.
  • Entry fee Rs. 300 per team.
  • Each member of the team must contain the identity card of his/her respected event.
  • The robot should not damage the arena.
  • The robot must not leave behind any of its parts during the run; else it will result in Disqualification.
  • Unethical behavior could lead to disqualification. Faculty coordinators have all the rights to take final decision for any matter during the event.
  • Judge's decision will be considered final.
  • Certificates will be given to all the participants.

    • *Rules may subject to change.

What are the rules for Circuit Making

Rules:


  • Teams of two participants only can contest for this event.
  • There will be total 2 rounds.
  • First round will be elimination round with ON SPOT PROBLEM STATEMENT from fields of electronics.
  • Second round will be circuit designing round in which actual application has to be made.
  • Both the rounds are open in nature, means you will be provided question sheets/components on the registration desk and you need to submit them back within the specified time limit.
  • There will be time bonus.
  • All the components and material required for the circuit will be provided by us.
  • Power supply and other testing devices like multimeter, CRO (if needed) and other tools will be made available in a shared hall.

 

CIRCUIT:- (Disclosed on time)

 

The application will be judged according to the following criteria:

  • Design of circuit. (10)
  • Compactness of design. (15)
  • Efficiency of the application. (15)
  • Desired output. (20)
  • Exploration of concepts/theories.(10)
  • Breadboarding skills/neatness of the implemented circuit. (10)
  • Additional functions/options available in the Application. (10)<
  • Contents of Chart Sheet. (10)
